Articles with their fact check score

Learn more about this fact checking
  • The rise of fake news and misinformation is increasingly recognised in many media sources.
  • Here you can check how factual each article seems based on its short description.
  • The given score is by a model made in IDIR: The Innovative Data Intelligence Research Laboratory. The score is between 0.0 (non-factual, opinionated or subjective) and 1.0 (factual). Here the score is visualised on a scale.
  • When the scale shows the dot on the right side of the scale, the more likely the content of the description is factual claims. The lower the score, so the more left the dot is, the more non-factual, opinionated and subjective the sentence seems.
